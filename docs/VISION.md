# Aperture Vision

## Purpose

Aperture is created first for people who enjoy nature, take photographs, and want to understand what appears in those photographs. It helps a person organise evidence, identify subjects, create observations, learn over time, and preserve a personal record of the natural world.

Aperture is useful on its own. Supporting science is an optional path chosen by the user, not a requirement for using the application.

## Aperture and NatureAI Next

**Aperture is the complete suite, user experience, data model, library format, and ecosystem.** The Aperture Library and its databases contain the user's authoritative Aperture data.

**NatureAI Next is the main software engine and integration layer.** It connects Aperture with open-source AI, taxonomy, mapping, storage, and future specialist technologies supplied by other parties. This boundary allows external components to evolve while Aperture's data and user workflows remain stable wherever practical.

## Core principles

### Personal and useful first

Every capability should provide value to the individual nature enthusiast before it is considered for broader scientific use.

### Offline first

Aperture is designed to remain useful at home, while travelling, and in places with limited or no connectivity. Network access extends acquisition and updates; it must not determine whether local data and installed capabilities can be used.

### The user owns the library

The user chooses where the Aperture Library is stored, how it is backed up, and whether selected information is ever exported. The personal library remains authoritative.

### Photographs are the evidence base

A photograph may contain multiple sightings: a bird, a tree, fungi, insects, or other subjects. Aperture preserves the photograph and its provenance as evidence, while separate observations can describe what was found in it.

### Modular capabilities

AI engines, maps, taxonomy, knowledge tools, and future modules can be enabled, disabled, installed, or omitted according to the user's interests and the limits of the available system. Capabilities extend the experience without changing ownership of the user's core data.

### AI assists

AI provides suggestions and enrichments. It does not replace the user's judgement or silently overwrite confirmed information.

### Long-term compatibility

Aperture should evolve without repeatedly reinventing its library. Migrations and compatibility plans are part of any feature that affects Aperture data.

## Future ecosystem

The long-term ecosystem may include tools and products with names that are decided later. The Vision describes directions rather than fixed product names or release promises.

### Lightweight field and mobile tools

Future tools may run on mobile devices, lightweight computers, portable systems, or specialised field hardware. They may contain only the regional maps, taxonomy, AI resources, and storage needed for a trip or field task.

They should support limited connectivity, offline work, camera or device import, and transfer by local network, cable, device, or storage card.

### Full personal systems

A fuller home or workstation installation may hold larger libraries, broader taxonomy, more extensive maps, stronger AI models, and deeper knowledge tools. Data collected by a lightweight field system can be imported while retaining provenance and observation history.

### Optional scientific contribution

A future scientific environment may receive selected, validated exports from personal Aperture systems. It may aggregate observations from many contributors, projects, posts, devices, and institutions.

Scientific tools may need very different models: migration over time for animals, rapid spatial and temporal expansion for outbreaks, deep taxonomic hierarchies for bacteria and strains, or repeated monitoring from several field posts. These capabilities belong in specialised modules, editions, services, or branches rather than being forced into one universal desktop workflow.

The science environment receives deliberate exports, not unrestricted access to personal libraries. Participation remains voluntary.

### Open architecture and branches

Aperture's architecture and development documentation are intentionally available so others can understand the decisions, create specialised branches, and build environments based on their own philosophy. Branches may diverge while respecting data ownership, modularity, interoperability, and compatibility wherever practical.

## A continuing project

Aperture is an ongoing project. Its scope may grow as the natural world, technology, scientific needs, and even future areas of exploration become part of it. Development proceeds step by step: first creating useful, dependable tools people can enjoy today, then adding optional capabilities without sacrificing the stable foundation.

> **Aperture begins with curiosity, grows through observation, and can contribute to discovery—always under the user's control.**

The Vision is intentionally broader than any individual release. Features described here may appear gradually, under different names, or in separate branches and products.
